Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi is known for his philosophy of nonviolence and his leadership in India's struggle for independence.

Here's a time line of Mahatma Gandhi's life:

  • October 2, 1869: Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi is born in Porbandar, Gujarat, India.
  • 1888: Gandhi travels to London, England, to study law.
  • 1893: Gandhi travels to South Africa for a legal case and witnesses racial discrimination, inspiring him to fight for justice.
  • 1894: Gandhi founds the Natal Indian Congress to advocate for the rights of Indian immigrants in South Africa.
  • 1906: Gandhi introduces the concept of Satyagraha (nonviolent resistance) during the protests against the Asiatic Registration Act in South Africa.
  • 1915: Gandhi returns to India and is hailed as a national leader.
  • 1919: Gandhi leads the Kheda Satyagraha, a nonviolent protest against British land taxes in Gujarat.
  • 1920: Gandhi becomes the leader of the Indian National Congress and initiates the Noncooperation Movement against British rule.
  • 1930: Gandhi leads the Salt March, a 240-mile march to protest the British salt monopoly, sparking a wave of civil disobedience across India.
  • 1932: Gandhi undertakes a hunger strike to protest the unequal treatment of Dalits (untouchables) and fights for their rights.
  • 1942: Gandhi launches the Quit India Movement, demanding an end to British rule in India.
  • 1947: India achieves independence from British rule, but the country is partitioned into India and Pakistan, leading to communal violence.
  • January 30, 1948: Mahatma Gandhi is assassinated in New Delhi by Nathuram Godse, a Hindu nationalist who disagreed with Gandhi's policies.
